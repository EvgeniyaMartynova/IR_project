Title: Rhodophaea formosa
Aspects: {'P. formosa'}

Rhodophaea formosa is a moth of the family Pyralidae. It is found in most of Europe.The wingspan is 20–23 millimetres (0.79–0.91 in). The moth flies in one generation from July to August.[1]The larvae feed on elm.


== Notes ==
^ The flight season refers to Belgium and the Netherlands. This may vary in other parts of the range.
