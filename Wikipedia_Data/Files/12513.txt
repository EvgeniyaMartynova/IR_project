Title: Oria musculosa
Aspects: {'S. flavescens'}

The Brighton Wainscot (Oria musculosa) is a moth of the family Noctuidae.
It is found in mainland Europe, north Africa and central Asia, with a separated population in South Africa.
It is nationally scarce in Britain where it may be confined to Salisbury Plain, and was pronounced extinct as a resident species by 2013.The wingspan is 28â€“34 mm.
The moth flies in July and August.
The larvae feed internally in the stems of cereal crops.
