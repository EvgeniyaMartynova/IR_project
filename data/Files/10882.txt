Title: Chrysoritis orientalis
Aspects: {'P. orientalis'}

Chrysoritis orientalis, the eastern opal, is a species of butterfly in the family Lycaenidae. It is endemic to South Africa, where it is found on the southern Drakensberg in KwaZulu-Natal.
The wingspan is 18–22 mm for males and 20–24 mm for females. Adults are on wing from October to January, with a peak in December. There is one generation per year.The larvae feed on Thesium species. They are attended to by Crematogaster liengmei ants.
