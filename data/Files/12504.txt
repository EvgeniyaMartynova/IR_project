Title: Synechodes exigua
Aspects: {'S. exigua'}

Synechodes exigua is a moth in the family Brachodidae. It was described by Kallies in 2004. It is found in India (Assam).
The wingspan is 14â€“16 mm for males and 15 mm for females. The forewings are black with a transverse yellow stripe near the costa and large yellow spots near the anal margin and the base. The hindwings are black with a yellow subbasal band.


== Etymology ==
The species name refers to the size of the species and is derived from exiguus (meaning small).
