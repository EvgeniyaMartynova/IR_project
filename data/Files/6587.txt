Title: S/2003 J 10
Aspects: {'J10'}

S/2003 J 10 is a retrograde irregular satellite of Jupiter. It was discovered by a team of astronomers from the University of Hawaii led by Scott S. Sheppard et al. in 2003.
S/2003 J 10 is about 2 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 22,731 Mm in 700.129 days, at an inclination of 164° to the ecliptic (166° to Jupiter's equator), in a retrograde direction and with an eccentricity of 0.3438.
It seems to belong to the Carme group.
This moon has not been seen since its discovery in 2003 and is currently considered lost.
