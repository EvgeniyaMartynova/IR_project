Title: Eleutherodactylus orientalis
Aspects: {'E. orientalis'}

Eleutherodactylus orientalis is also known as Oriental robber frog,is a species of frog in the Eleutherodactylidae family endemic to Cuba. Its natural habitat is subtropical or tropical moist lowland forests.
It is threatened by habitat loss.


== Discovery ==
Eleutherodactylus orientalis averaging length 13.3 mm (0.52 in) in females and12.5 mm (0.49 in) in males.It is endemic to Cuba. It is known only from isolated localities in the Sagua-Baracoa Mountains, Oriente Province, Eastern Cuba, at elevations between 100 and 565 m above sea level. It is a terrestrial frog, often found under leaf litter in hardwood forests of limestone soil, and sometimes on coffee and cacao plantations.
