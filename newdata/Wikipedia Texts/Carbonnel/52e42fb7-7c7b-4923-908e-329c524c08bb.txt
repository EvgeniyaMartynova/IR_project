Delillers Carbonnel

Delillers Carbonnel (born 1654) was a British banker who was Governor of the Bank of England from 1740 to 1741. 
He was born in London, the son of Gillaume Carbonnel, a French Merchant.He was Deputy Governor of the Bank of England from 1738 to 1740, when he replaced Thomas Cooke as Governor, being succeeded in 1741 by Stamp Brooksbank.
