Robert B. Hauser

Robert B. Hauser, A.S.C., (March 25, 1919, Spokane, Washington - July 8, 1994, Los Angeles, California) was an American cinematographer that worked on more than 70 television and film projects from 1958 to his death in 1994, at age 75.


== Biography ==


=== Film and Television ===
For a period of 10 years from 1958 to 1968 he worked mainly in television as director of photography. From 1958 to 1959 he worked on 12 episodes of Man with a Camera that starred Charles Bronson. In 1960 he did 4 episodes of The Untouchables  Others he worked on were Combat!, Breaking Point, Peyton Place,  The Man from U.N.C.L.E., Mission: Impossible and Trapper John, M.D. etc. 
From 1968 to 1981 he worked on many films including The Odd Couple, Riot, Mean Dog Blues and Killjoy 


=== Nominations ===
He was nominated twice for Primetime Emmy awards. Once in 1963 for TV series Combat and again in 1978 for Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry.
