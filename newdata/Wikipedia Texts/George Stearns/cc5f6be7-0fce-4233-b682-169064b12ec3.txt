George Stearns (politician)

George McClellan (Mac) Stearns (17 December 1901 – 9 January 1979) was a Progressive Conservative party member of the House of Commons of Canada. He was an industrialist by career.
Stearns was born at Lac-Mégantic, Quebec. He was the older brother Édouard Stearns and the uncle of renowned historian Sir Peter Stearns. After an unsuccessful attempt to win the Compton—Frontenac riding in the 1957 federal election, Stearns was elected in the 1958 election. He served one term, the 24th Canadian Parliament, before leaving federal office and did not campaign for re-election in 1962. In 1979, he died aged 77 in Lac Megantic.
