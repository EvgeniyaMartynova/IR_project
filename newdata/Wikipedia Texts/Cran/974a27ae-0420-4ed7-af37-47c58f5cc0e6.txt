Representative Council of France's Black Associations

The Conseil représentatif des associations noires de France (CRAN, Representative Council of France's Black Associations) is a federation of African and Afro-Caribbean associations in France, created in November 2005.The first chairperson of CRAN was the Bénin-born Patrick Lozès, son of former Beninese Minister Gabriel Lozès, elected in 2005 and reelected in 2008. In November 2011 he was succeeded by the Martinique-born Louis-Georges Tin, a LGBT activist.
