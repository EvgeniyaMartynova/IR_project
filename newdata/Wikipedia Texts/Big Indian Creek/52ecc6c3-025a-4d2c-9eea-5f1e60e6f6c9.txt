Big Indian Creek (Georgia)

Big Indian Creek is a stream in the U.S. state of Georgia. It is a tributary to the Ocmulgee River.Big Indian Creek was so named in honor of Tustinugee, a Creek chieftain, on account of his large stature.Big Indian Creeks source is SouthWestern Peach County and Macon County. It flows into Houston County at I-75 where it captures Bay Creek. It flows through Perry Ga in an Eastern direction. It crosses US 341 South and captures Mossy Creek. It enters the Ocmulgee River as a tributary in the Oaky Woods Wildlife Management Area.
