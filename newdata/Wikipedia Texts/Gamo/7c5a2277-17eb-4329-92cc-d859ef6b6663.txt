Gamo people

Gamo is the name of the Ethiopian ethnic group, who speak the Gamo language. The name "Gamo" means a lion, which refers to their legacy and one of cushitic peoples; along with the Goffa, they gave their names to the former Gamo-Gofa province of Ethiopia. The 2007 Ethiopian national census reported that 16,107,163 people (or 16.5% of the ethiopia population) identified themselves as Gamos, of whom 6,141,233 were urban inhabitants. The Southern Nations, Nationalities, and People's Region is home to 64.5% of this people.The Gamo contribute much of the fruit including banana, mango, apple and papaya, to the capital city of Ethiopia, Addis Ababa. Their religion is Christianity.
